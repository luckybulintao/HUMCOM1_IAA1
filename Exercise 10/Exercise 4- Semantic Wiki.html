TARANTULAS

Introduction to Tarantulas

Tarantulas are arachnids related to spiders but tarantulas grow much larger and can weigh up to 1 to 3 ounces. Tarantulas could live up to 30 years depending in the species and its environment, they can also reach a legspan of 11 inches depending on the species. Tarantulas are carnivores and also insectivores which preys on insects, birds, snakes, and other arachnids. The scientific name of a tarantulas is "Theraposidae". There are two kinds of tarantulas which are old world and new world tarantulas. Old world tarantulas are from the eastern hemisphere and they more aggresive and prone to biting. New world tarantulas came from the western hemisphere and they are more docile compared to old world tarantulas and are equiped with urticating hairs.

History of Tarantulas

There are many species of tarantulas which where discovered first in the year 1800's. These discoveries continued to grow as zoologist and scientist continue to discover more and more tarantula species and genus. As years went by, these discoveries have continued to grow. Habitat

There are hundreds of tarantula species found in most of the world's tropical, subtropical, and arid regions. They vary in color and behavior according to their specific environments. Generally, however, tarantulas are burrowers that live in the ground.

Molting

Tarantulas periodically shed their external skeletons in a process called molting. In the process, they also replace internal organs, such as female genitalia and stomach lining, and even regrow lost appendages.

Hunting

Tarantulas are slow and deliberate movers, but accomplished nocturnal predators. Insects are their main prey, but they also target bigger game, including frogs, toads, and mice. The South American bird-eating spider, as it name suggests, is even able to prey upon small birds.
